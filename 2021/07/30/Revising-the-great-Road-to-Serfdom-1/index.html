Revising the great Road to Serfdom(1)
Publish: 2021/7/30   
  • Economics Classics Hayek

Nowadays it is common sense that Friedrich August Von Hayek is a great economist and his masterpiece The Road to Serfdom is on the must-read list of every student study economic, politics and sociology.

However, this is not the case when this book was firstly published in 1944. After the trauma of two times of world war and huge economic turmoil, people began to question classical liberalism and to treat socialism as a better alternative. Left-wing ideas were the mainstream, among such current of thought Hayek stood out and appealed:” Do not go the road of the command economy, that’s a road of serfdom!” We can imagine how much criticisms were there on him.

Events that happened in the 20th century approved Hayek’s thoughts, especially after the new phenomenon of stagflation when inflation and economic recession occurred spontaneously after the 1973 oil crisis. Hayek’s liberalism and Milton Freedman’s monetarism were revalued and become the principle of the Reagan and Thatcher government; they brought a golden age of economic growth.

Nowadays the collapse of the Soviet Union and the miracle of China’s economic growth after it introduced free-market again supported Hayek. What is the magic of Hayek? How did he reemphasise and reform classical liberalism?

I think there are some important points that he made.

  1. The free-market economy is the most efficient and ideal allocation system that human beings could find so far.

  2. The economic freedom is the foundation of political freedom.

  3. Freedom is only possible under the rule by law

  4. Laws are telos(purpose), not means. Governments should OBEY the law to govern the society instead of USE the law.

  5. Laissez faire is detrimental.

  6. Democracy is merely a means to realize the liberty of individuals and stability of the society, it is not the telos.
