Can we survive our deaths?(1)
Publish: 2021/8/22   
  • Philosophy

(It is the notes I made for the open class of professor Shelly of philosophy in Yale)

Is there any chance that we can still survive after we died?

The objection sounds quite obvious and easy to understand. By the definition of death means the end of life, it is just like asking what happens after the end of a movie? Can we still eat the food when all the food on the plate has been eaten?

The term “survives”, and “death” cannot appear at the same time, there is an old classic joke says: “ If a plane exactly on the border of America and Canada, where do you bury the survivors?” The answer is, we don’t bury the survivors

But these objections typically refer to the body death, to make the question precise, we shall ask: “ Do I still exist after my body died?” To answer this question, we must figure out what am I? What kind of thing is a person and what does it mean for it to die?”

There are two main positions of this question. One thinks that a person is a combination of a body and something else – the mind, or shall I call it, the soul. An immaterial substance. it is a metaphysical substance and must be understood in nonphysical terms. It is the dualist view.

The other view thinks that a person is just a certain kind of material object that can do some amazing things other objects cannot. It is the physicalist view.

Death to the first position means the severing of the super tight connection between the body and the soul.

In this point of view, there is a chance that we could still exist after the death of our body, or maybe not because body and soul, they work in pairs, if the pair no longer exist (one part of the pair died) then person no longer exists.

So here brings the first question in this point of view: Are soul and body distinct? Followed by this question we can continue to ask: Can the soul survive the destruction of the body? (Maybe the body can bring the soul away when it dies as they interact tightly) If it can, for how long?
